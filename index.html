Carlos Martínez-Núñez

I am a community ecologist interested in understanding how global change drivers affect the spatiotemporal distribution of species, their interactions, and how these impacts scale up to modify ecosystem properties, functions, or services. I am currently immersed in various captivating projects that revolve around conservation ecology, with a special emphasis on the ecology and conservation of insect pollinators across agricultural and climatic gradients.

My contribution to science and society to date has focused on three key pillars: Agroecology, Urban ecology, and Macroecology.
